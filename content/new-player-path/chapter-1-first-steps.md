# Chapter 1: First Steps

Welcome to War Thunder! This chapter will help you understand the basics of the game interface and controls.

## What is War Thunder?

War Thunder is a military vehicle combat game featuring:
- **Ground Forces**: Tanks and armored vehicles
- **Aviation**: Fighter aircraft, bombers, and attackers
- **Naval Forces**: Ships and boats
- **Realistic Physics**: Ballistics, armor penetration, and vehicle characteristics

## Understanding the Main Menu

When you first launch War Thunder, you'll see several key areas:

### 1. Game Modes

War Thunder offers several game modes:
- **Arcade Battles (AB)**: Faster-paced, forgiving physics
- **Realistic Battles (RB)**: More realistic, requires more skill
- **Simulator Battles (SB)**: Maximum realism, challenging

**Recommendation for Beginners**: Start with Arcade Battles to learn the basics!

### 2. Research Trees

Each nation has a research tree showing all available vehicles:
- Vehicles are organized by type (tanks, aircraft, etc.)
- You research vehicles in a specific order
- Each vehicle requires Research Points (RP) to unlock

### 3. Hangar

Your hangar is where you:
- View your owned vehicles
- Customize your lineups
- Check vehicle statistics
- Equip modifications

## Basic Controls (Ground Forces)

### Movement
- **W/A/S/D**: Forward/Left/Backward/Right
- **Mouse**: Rotate turret (in arcade mode)
- **Shift**: Faster movement (highway gear)
- **Space**: Brake

### Combat
- **Left Click**: Fire main gun
- **Right Click**: Zoom/aiming view
- **R**: Reload
- **V**: Change view (third person, gunner view, etc.)
- **X**: Stabilize gun (for stabilized vehicles)

## Basic Controls (Aviation)

### Flight
- **W/S**: Pitch up/down
- **A/D**: Roll left/right
- **Q/E**: Yaw left/right
- **Shift**: Increase throttle
- **Ctrl**: Decrease throttle
- **Space**: Fire weapons

## Understanding the Battle Interface

During a match, you'll see:

### Minimap
- Shows the map layout
- Displays enemy and friendly positions
- Helps with navigation and positioning

### Damage Indicator
- Red indicators show where you're being hit from
- Use this to identify enemy locations

### Vehicle Information
- Your current vehicle name
- Ammunition count
- Crew status

## First Steps Checklist

Before moving to the next chapter, make sure you:

- [ ] Launched your first battle (Arcade mode recommended)
- [ ] Understand basic movement controls
- [ ] Can aim and fire your weapon
- [ ] Understand the minimap
- [ ] Know how to check vehicle information

## Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Rushing into battle**: Take your time, observe the map
2. **Ignoring the minimap**: Always check where enemies might be
3. **Not using cover**: Don't drive in open fields
4. **Solo gameplay**: Try to work with your team
