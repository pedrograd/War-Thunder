## 1. Economy Fundamentals

### Silver Lions (SL)

**What Are Silver Lions?**
SL is War Thunder's primary currency, used for purchasing vehicles, repairs, crew training, ammunition, and modifications.

**Earning Silver Lions**:
- Battle performance (kills, assists, caps, etc.)
- Time alive and active
- Vehicle tier and modifications
- Premium account bonus
- Premium vehicles bonus

**Spending Silver Lions**:
- Vehicle purchases
- Repair costs (per spawn)
- Crew training and expert/ace qualifications
- Ammunition purchases
- Vehicle modifications

**Critical Concept**: SL can be earned or lost. Managing SL is crucial for sustainable progression.

---

### Research Points (RP)

**What Are Research Points?**
RP is earned through battles and used to research new vehicles and modifications.

**Earning Research Points**:
- Battle performance (kills, assists, caps, etc.)
- Time alive and active
- Vehicle tier and modifications
- Premium account bonus
- Premium vehicles bonus
- Talisman bonuses

**Using Research Points**:
- Researching new vehicles
- Researching vehicle modifications
- Cannot be spent on purchases

**Critical Concept**: RP is separate from SL. You need both to progress.

## 2. Economic Costs

### Repair Costs

**What Are Repair Costs?**
Every vehicle has a repair cost that's deducted when you spawn it, even if you don't die.

**Repair Cost Characteristics**:
- Increases with BR (higher BR = higher cost)
- Varies by vehicle type
- Premium vehicles often have lower costs
- Costs are deducted per spawn

**Economic Impact**:
- High repair costs can drain SL quickly
- Dying early with high repair costs = SL loss
- Low repair costs allow more aggressive play
- Understanding costs prevents bankruptcy

**How to Manage**:
- Check repair costs before spading vehicles
- Consider costs when choosing BR ranges
- Play carefully at high-tier (high costs)
- Use lower repair cost vehicles to recover SL

---

### Ammunition Costs

**What Are Ammunition Costs?**
Different shell types cost different amounts. Firing expensive shells can add up.

**Cost Characteristics**:
- Basic shells (AP, APHE) are cheaper
- Advanced shells (APDS, APFSDS, HEAT-FS) are expensive
- Firing many expensive shells = significant cost
- Shell costs vary by tier and type

**Economic Impact**:
- Firing 50+ expensive shells per match adds up
- Shell choice affects profitability
- Sometimes cheaper shells are more economical

**How to Manage**:
- Use appropriate shells for situation
- Don't waste expensive shells
- Mix shell types based on targets
- Consider economy when choosing shells

---

### Crew Training

**What Is Crew Training?**
Training crews improves performance but costs SL.

**Training Costs**:
- Basic crew training (required to use vehicle)
- Expert qualification (significant improvement)
- Ace qualification (maximum improvement)
- Costs increase with tier and vehicle

**Economic Impact**:
- Well-trained crews significantly improve performance
- Crew training costs can be substantial
- Important to prioritize which crews to train

**How to Manage**:
- Train crews for main lineup first
- Expert qualification is often worth it
- Ace qualification is expensive, consider carefully
- Focus training on important skills

## 4. Premium Time and Premium Vehicles

### Premium Time

**What Is Premium Time?**
Premium account status doubles RP and SL earnings.

**When It Makes Sense**:
- If you play regularly
- When you want faster progression
- When you have disposable income
- If you enjoy the game long-term

**Economic Consideration**:

- Consider time-limited deals
- Not required for progression

### Premium Vehicles

**What Are Premium Vehicles?**
Vehicles that earn more RP/SL than tech tree equivalents.

**When They Make Sense**:
- After understanding what you want
- When you enjoy a specific vehicle
- When they fit your playstyle
- When they help grind specific tech trees

**Economic Consideration**:

- Effect: Increased RP/SL earnings

**Conservative Advice**:
- Wait until you know what you want

Items that double RP earnings on a specific vehicle.

**When They Make Sense**:
- When you love a specific vehicle
- When vehicle fits your playstyle
- When you'll use vehicle long-term
- When vehicle is effective for you

## 8. Case Study: Two Approaches to Economy

Understanding economic principles is easier with concrete examples. Here's a comparison of two different approaches:

### Player A: The Top-Tier Rushed

**Approach**: Rushed to top tier with one MBT, plays top-tier exclusively.

**Lineup**: Single top-tier MBT, no backup vehicles, no SPAA, no CAS.

**Economic Results**:
- Dies early in most matches (limited experience at top tier)
- Loses SL on most matches (high repair costs, low battle activity)
- Can't afford to repair vehicle consistently
- Forced to play lower tiers to recover SL
- Slow progression due to poor performance

**Why It Fails**: 
- No backup vehicles = low battle activity
- High repair costs + poor performance = SL loss
- Single vehicle = limited spawn options
- Rushed progression = lack of fundamentals

### Player B: The Mid-Tier Specialist

**Approach**: Masters mid-tier (5.7-6.7), builds strong lineups, focuses on fundamentals.

**Lineup**: Multiple vehicles at same BR, includes MBT, light tank, SPAA, and CAS options.

**Economic Results**:
- Consistent performance (mastered BR range)
- Profits on most matches (reasonable repair costs, good battle activity)
- Can afford repairs and crew training
- Sustainable progression
- Strong fundamentals transfer to higher tiers

**Why It Succeeds**:
- Full lineup = high battle activity
- Reasonable costs + good performance = SL profit
- Multiple vehicles = flexible spawn options
- Mastered fundamentals = better performance

> **Economy Insight:** Player B's approach is sustainable and effective. They build strong fundamentals while maintaining a healthy economy. When they advance to higher tiers, they do so with experience and economic stability.

### The Budget Grinding Framework

Use this step-by-step framework to optimize your economy:

**Step 1: Assess Your Current Situation**
- What BR range are you playing?
- Are you profiting or losing SL?
- What's your average battle activity?
- Do you have full lineups?

**Step 2: Identify Problems**
- Are repair costs too high for your performance?
- Are you dying too early?
- Do you lack backup vehicles?
- Are you playing above your skill level?

**Step 3: Adjust BR Range**
- If losing SL, drop to lower BR
- Find BR where you can consistently profit
- Master that BR before advancing

**Step 4: Build Complete Lineups**
- Add backup vehicles at same BR
- Include SPAA for air defense
- Consider CAS options
- Keep BR spread tight (within 0.7 BR)

**Step 5: Focus on Survival**
- Improve positioning
- Use cover effectively
- Don't take unnecessary risks
- Retreat when outmatched

**Step 6: Monitor and Adjust**
- Track your SL balance
- Adjust BR if needed
- Build lineups gradually
- Focus on fundamentals

> **Pro Tip:** This framework works at any BR. Apply it consistently, and you'll maintain a healthy economy while improving your skills.
